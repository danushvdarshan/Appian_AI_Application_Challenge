--------------------------------------------------
Section 1: Overview
--------------------------------------------------
The National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) provides insurance coverage
to property owners for flood-related damages. Claims must be filed in
accordance with federal and state-specific requirements.

--------------------------------------------------
Section 2: Covered Flood Events
--------------------------------------------------
Flooding is defined as a general and temporary condition of partial or
complete inundation of normally dry land areas due to overflow of inland
or tidal waters.

--------------------------------------------------
Section 4: Claims Submission Requirements
--------------------------------------------------

4.1 Initial Notification  
Policyholders must notify their insurer as soon as reasonably possible
after a flood event has occurred.

4.2 Proof of Loss Requirement (Florida Specific)  
For flood insurance claims filed in the state of Florida, the policyholder
must submit a signed and sworn Proof of Loss within thirty (30) days of
the date of loss. Failure to comply may result in claim denial.

4.3 Documentation  
Supporting documentation may include photographs, repair estimates,
and contractor invoices.

--------------------------------------------------
Section 6: Compliance and Audits
--------------------------------------------------
All flood claims are subject to post-payment audits to ensure compliance
with federal regulations and internal policy guidelines.
